Co-op browser RTS skirmish. Working name TBD. 12-15 minute target match.
Two players, online, vs. an asymmetric AI enemy. Players have separate but adjacent bases sharing a frontline. Resources are shared and pooled — gathered by worker units from nodes spread across a small map (think AoE2 Arabia at ~half scale). Both players can spend from the shared pool freely.
Resources are unlikely to truly deplete, but the good nodes are spread outward — so to scale up, you have to expand, and expanding exposes workers and frontline. This creates a natural pressure curve as the match progresses: early game is about establishing economy and protecting close nodes, mid-game is about pushing outward and contesting territory, late-game is about whether your built-up forces can break the enemy or you fall to their escalation.
Building is continuous and real-time. Structures take 15-45 seconds to build depending on type. One tech tier — you start basic, mid-game you unlock advanced units/upgrades. ~6-8 unit types per faction.
The AI enemy occupies the far side of the map with its own base and forces. It doesn't play the same game as you — it escalates on its own schedule, sends probes early and committed attacks later. You can attack its base to win early (preserves AoE2's "we can push them" decision), or weather the storm to the late game. The AI is asymmetric: simple legible behaviors, scripted patterns, but tunable difficulty.
Loss condition: both bases fall (shared loss). Win condition: destroy the AI base/objective, OR survive a final boss-wave at the time limit (TBD which feels better).
